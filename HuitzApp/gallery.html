El Museo de la Cuidad de Mexico built in 1961. It is located at Pino Suarez 30, a few blocks south of the Zocalo. On what was the Iztapalapa Causeway, it is near where Hernán Cortés and Moctezuma II met for the first time. It hosts Nuahtl speaking courses for Huitz.
El Mueseo de Tequila y Mezcal built in 2010. It is located on Plaza Garibaldi and it provides history courses and nightime events for Huitz that delve into the origins and production process of Mexico’s two most popular distilled agave drinks.
El Museo del Pulque y las Pulquerías , which is located on Avenida Hidalgo in the Historic Distric of Mexico City. It offers Pulque-related courses, which will tap into Tequila's and Mezcal's Aztec roots.
El Mueso del Templo Mayor, started in 1987, showcases the ruins of the main temple of the Mexica people in their capital city of Tenochtitlan. It was originally built during the mid-twelvth century and is now located in the now Seminario 8 located in Mexico City's Historic Distric. Its architectural style belongs to the late Postclassic period of Mesoamerica and the temple was named the Huēyi Teōcalli in the Nahuatl language. It will host Nuahtl speaking courses for Huitz.
