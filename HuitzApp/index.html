Raíces

Here at Huitz, we take pride in paying homage to Tequila's Aztec roots, Pulque. It features five distilling processes: Pulque, Spanish Mezcal, Añejo, Reposado, and Blanco, which offers a taste of history through every sip. For more information about rich history of tequila and how each is (traditionally) meant to be enjoyed visit liquor.com.

Inspiración

My inspiration for Huitz Tequila was based from an Aztec calendar necklace that my father would wear every day. As I grew older, he would share stories of the Aztec gods, and warrior-like, Pulque drinking people. He explained how they revered one god above all else which was Huitzilopochtli, the god of war, sun, and human sacrifice and the patron of the city of Tenochtitlan. Hence, where the name Huitz came from, why my bottle is shaped like the Templo Mayor, and why my brand has other Azteec symbolisms interwoven into the design. I wanted a product that paid homage to the Aztecs, because without their Pulque, Tequila would not be enjoyed cross-culturally and without their language and culture others based from it would cease to exist.
